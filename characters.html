Character Design

From realistic reference to a unified visual identity.

Asym \ phaseThe characters in Asym \ phase are not designed as isolated figures. They belong to the same visual system as the posters, title structure, and staged scenes, carrying era, mood, and emotional transition through costume, silhouette, and posture.

This page gathers selected materials from the current development stage: realistic references, stylized sheets, and turnaround-based views used to stabilize the project’s visual language.

Adult Character

The later phase: memory, restraint, and forward movement

Stylized Sheet

Adult protagonist

The adult figure carries the project’s later emotional phase. He begins from a realistic period reference and is then translated into a more unified stylized language, keeping formal steam-train-era clothing, a controlled silhouette, and a calmer, more self-possessed presence.

In the poster system and model showcase, this character becomes the visual anchor of the warmer stage: the point where farewell has already happened, but forward movement has become steadier.

Childhood Phase

The boy and Nello as the earlier emotional structure

Stylized Pairing

Boy and sheepdog

The childhood phase is built around the boy and Nello as a paired presence rather than two separate designs. Together they establish the warmth, motion, and companionship that make the project’s later loss meaningful.

At the same time, this stage is not treated as simple innocence. As in the childhood poster, companionship remains on the surface while the deeper structure of separation has already begun to enter the image.

Design Logic

Character design as part of the narrative structure

01

Era and silhouette

Costume and overall shape establish the steam-train-era atmosphere while keeping the characters recognizable across poster, model, and sheet presentation.

02

Realistic to stylized

Realistic reference is used as the starting point, then translated into a stylized language that stays consistent with the project’s wider visual world.

03

Emotional function

Each character carries a phase of the story: companionship in childhood, retrospection in adulthood, and the distance between them.

Current Scope

A visual development stage rather than a full production asset set

At the current stage, the character work focuses on visual development, presentation, and symbolic consistency rather than full animation-ready production. The materials shown here establish identity, proportion, costume direction, and visual tone, while topology rebuilding, rigging, and further animation-asset development remain later stages.
