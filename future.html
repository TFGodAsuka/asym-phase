Future Development

The current version is complete as a phase, not as a final ending.

The graduation version of Asym \ phase stops at the part that is already the most mature: character design, poster design, and model showcase. It does not try to force the entire project into a fully finished animated short within a limited production period.

What remains unfinished is not the core idea, but the next layer of production. The current stage establishes the visual language, symbolic structure, and emotional tone strongly enough for later expansion.

Why It Stops Here

This stage focuses on visual development rather than full production

The current model work uses AI-assisted high-detail display assets and Blender-based reconstruction to develop composition, atmosphere, lighting, and narrative presentation. More demanding production steps such as topology rebuilding, clothing separation, and rigging belong to a later phase, because they require a much longer and more technical workflow.

For that reason, the graduation version is not a reduced idea. It is the most concentrated and controllable form of the project at this stage.

Possible Next Steps

Three directions the project can continue from here

01

Expand the childhood side

Add the young protagonist and Nello as a more complete visual pair, including further design work and high-detail display presentation.

02

Build animation-ready character assets

Reconstruct the current character into a neutral production model with cleaner topology, separated clothing layers, independent hair and accessories, and basic rigging tests.

03

Move toward an animated short or concept film

Continue from the existing design system into storyboard development, shot design, animation tests, and possibly an AI-animation-based or concept-short direction.

Later Development

From visual project to moving-image language

Once the characters and visual structure become stable enough, the project can move further into storyboard, shot continuity, atmosphere passages, and fragment-based animation experiments. At that point, Asym \ phase would begin to shift from a visual development project toward a concept short or a more complete animated work.

Even in that later form, the tone should remain restrained. Its strength is not spectacle, but the quiet pressure of memory, farewell, and forward movement.

Closing Note

This website records one completed phase of an ongoing work

The project is not unfinished in the sense of lacking direction. It is unfinished only in the sense that its next form has been left open on purpose.

In that sense, Asym \ phase stays consistent with its own title: each phase stands on its own, while still leaning toward another one.
