Model Showcase

From poster language to staged space in Blender.

This part of Asym \ phase does not treat the posters as flat final images. Their internal structure is rebuilt in Blender through character placement, symbolic props, title-space relationships, and controlled lighting.

The current models are used as display assets rather than animation-ready production meshes. What matters here is spatial reconstruction, visual atmosphere, and narrative presentation.

Stage Reconstruction I

Childhood Stage Reconstruction

The childhood reconstruction brings together the boy, Nello, the tilted kerosene lamp, the branching path, and the oversized title structure within one staged composition.

It keeps the warmth of companionship on the surface, while the slanted lantern and divided ground quietly carry the earlier tension of imbalance and separation.

Stage Reconstruction II

Adult Stage Reconstruction

The adult reconstruction shifts into a steadier and warmer emotional state. Here, the composition becomes clearer, the figure more self-possessed, and the model presentation stays aligned with the poster’s restrained farewell.

The scene is built to support that later phase of the project: looking back without remaining there, and continuing forward with a quieter sense of resolve.

Blender Process

Scene assembly inside the working file.

The working file records how the visual system is rebuilt through model placement, symbolic objects, title structure, camera framing, and lighting control.

In this stage, Blender is used to reconstruct atmosphere and composition rather than to produce fully rigged animation assets.
