Poster System

Two stages of one narrative.

Asym \ phaseThe two posters of Asym \ phase do not repeat the same emotion. The childhood image stays with the moment before separation is fully understood, while the adult image returns after that passage, when looking back no longer prevents moving forward. Together, they complete one structure: premonition and farewell, beginning and aftermath.

Phase I · Premonition

Before Separation

The childhood poster is not about relief after farewell, but unease before it. The boy and Nello are still running together, yet the image already suggests that this moment will not remain unchanged.

Here, the lantern takes over the role of the “\” symbol. Leaning against the title, it turns into a sign of imbalance and early separation; the path beneath them continues that tension, keeping companionship on the surface while quietly dividing the space below.

Phase II · Farewell

After Passage

The adult poster stands at a later point in the same emotional structure. Here, the farewell has already happened, and what remains is not collapse, but a restrained way of looking back while continuing forward.

The marigold replaces the “\” symbol, the composition becomes steadier, and the atmosphere turns warmer. If the childhood poster is a sign that separation has begun to enter the image, this one is its answer: not denial of the past, but the strength to carry it and still move on.

One poster points to the stage that is still being entered; the other looks back on the stage that has already been crossed. Together, they turn Asym \ phase into a complete visual pair rather than two isolated images.
