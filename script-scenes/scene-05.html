Parallel Interior

Quiet transition from the previous scene. Fixed medium shot inside a train compartment.

A well-dressed adult man sits alone. His expression is worn, his gaze dim, his presence composed but exhausted.

Table Setup

Before him lies a sheet of writing paper. Beside it sits a pot of marigolds.

The arrangement is still, deliberate, and heavy with unspoken thought.

Despair Draft

Close-up: his hand is writing.

The page is filled with words of despair. Visible fragments read:

“...I see no way out. The world is too heavy...”

Petal Interruption

A marigold petal drifts down quietly and lands on the back of his hand.

He freezes for a moment and stops writing.

Light Arc

At that instant, a trembling trace of light flashes across the valley outside the window.

Its trajectory perfectly overlaps with the remembered arc of the kerosene lamp falling through the night in childhood.

Recognition

Close-up: the adult man’s eyes.

The light from the past becomes a key, suddenly opening the sealed chamber of memory and feeling.

The despair in his face gives way to astonishment, warmth, and a nearly impossible recognition.

Rewriting

Without hesitation, he crumples the page filled with despair.

He takes up a fresh sheet and begins to write quickly. At the edge of his mouth, a faint smile appears — weak, distant, but real.

Fade Out

The camera pulls back and rises from the close-up of the writing hand toward the carriage ceiling lamp.

The image gradually darkens into transition.
